## The setup

A hackathon compresses every design problem into one weekend. No time for
revisions, no room for ambiguity, and developers who start building before the
design is finished, because they have to.

## The move

The highest-leverage thing I made all weekend was also the least glamorous: a
one-page visual language with the palette, type, spacing, and component states,
produced in the first hours and treated as law afterward.

<Sidenote n={1}>A design system's value is inversely proportional to how much
time you have. With no time to review each other's work, the system reviews it
for you.</Sidenote>

I produced the project's direction graphics alongside it, so the pitch deck,
the interface, and the repository README all spoke one language by demo time.

<Callout kind="finding">
Three people built screens that read as one person's work. Under time
pressure, coherence is the differentiator; most hackathon projects lose
theirs to committee.
</Callout>

## The takeaway

This weekend is my thesis about design systems in miniature: they are
operations tools as much as design artifacts. The palette was never the
point. The agreement was.
