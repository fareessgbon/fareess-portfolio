## The setup

Many high school students feel unprepared for university, especially when it comes to budgeting, time management, self-care, and building new connections.

## The move

Our team created an interactive choose-your-own-adventure game that guides students through realistic university scenarios. I helped design the user experience in Figma and worked with the team through Discord to organize ideas, divide responsibilities, and meet our deadline.

<Sidenote n={1}>Designed during the Black Wings Hackathon in collaboration with a four-person remote team. My contribution focused on UX design, scenario development, and translating complex university challenges into an accessible Figma prototype.</Sidenote>

I produced the project's direction graphics alongside it, so the pitch deck,
the interface, and the repository README all spoke one language by demo time.

<Callout kind="finding">
Students are more likely to engage with university preparation when practical skills—such as budgeting, time management, and self-care—are presented through realistic, interactive decisions rather than traditional advice.
</Callout>

## The takeaway

The project showed me how thoughtful design and teamwork can make complex information more engaging and accessible for students
