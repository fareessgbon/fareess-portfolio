## The brief

Planted Souls needed a website that matched a brand living mostly in its
founder's head. The real design problem was extraction before execution:
getting the brand out of conversation and into a form a build could follow.

## How it went

**Vision board first.** Before a single screen, we built a shared Figma board
of brand colours, type directions, and references, reviewed live with the CEO
until “that's it” replaced “sort of.”

**Then high-fidelity mockups.** I translated that direction into complete page
layouts using the agreed colours, typography, imagery, and visual style. This
made it easier to review real screens instead of making decisions from abstract
descriptions.

**Then the navigation pass.** We walked through the existing site's dead ends
and ambiguous labels together, then reorganized the experience around how a
first-time visitor would explore services, learn about the brand, and get
started.

<Callout kind="method">
Working founder-to-designer taught me to treat alignment as a deliverable with
its own artifacts. The vision board mattered as much as the mockups it
produced.
</Callout>

## What stuck with me

Small-client work is a masterclass in listening. The CEO knew the brand better
than I ever could. My job was building the instrument that let that knowledge
become a website.
